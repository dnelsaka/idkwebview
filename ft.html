Global markets rebound as central banks signal pause in rate cycle

Investors rotate back into equities after Federal Reserve chair hints at data-dependent approach following three consecutive hikes.

By Staff Reporter  |   |  4 min read

Global equity markets staged a broad-based recovery on Thursday as investors digested comments from Federal Reserve chair suggesting the central bank was prepared to pause its aggressive tightening cycle pending incoming economic data.

The S&P 500 rose 1.4 per cent in early New York trading, while the technology-heavy Nasdaq Composite added 1.9 per cent. In Europe, the Stoxx 600 index climbed 0.8 per cent, led by gains in financial and consumer discretionary stocks.

The yield on the two-year US Treasury note, which is particularly sensitive to monetary policy expectations, fell 12 basis points to 4.67 per cent — its sharpest single-day decline since March — as traders priced in a lower probability of a further rate increase at the June meeting.
